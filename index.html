Core Principle

League is fundamentally a turn-based strategy game disguised as real-time action. Each player gets "turns" to act based on their wave states and positioning. Understanding this principle changes everything about how we approach the game.

Tempo

Having initiative - you act, they react

What is Tempo?

Quick Definition:

Having initiative - you act, they react

Detailed Definition:

Tempo is who controls the pacing and timing of the game. When you have tempo, you can make proactive plays that force the enemy team to react to you. The team with tempo gets to choose the battlefield and timing, while the team without tempo must respond to threats rather than create them.

Key Rule

One Action for One Action

‚úì Kill enemy laner ‚Üí Push wave (uses up tempo = back to even)

‚úó Stay for plates after kill = extra action ‚Üí Enemy gains tempo when you recall

Tempo Scenarios

‚úì Gaining Tempo

  • ‚Ä¢ Get a kill and immediately push wave
  • ‚Ä¢ Force enemy recall and take their jungle camps
  • ‚Ä¢ Win a trade and roam to help jungle
  • ‚Ä¢ Clear wave faster and contest scuttle

‚úó Losing Tempo

  • ‚Ä¢ Get killed and enemy pushes wave
  • ‚Ä¢ Overstay and get ganked
  • ‚Ä¢ Recall at bad timing
  • ‚Ä¢ Chase too far and miss farm

Turns & Rounds

Using lane priority to create opportunities

Lane Priority

You gain lane priority when you push/crash wave under enemy tower, essentially locking your opponent or making them lose gold & XP.

Turn

When you have time before the next wave arrives - this is how you use your priority window.

Round

The time between when you're fresh off base until your next reset/recall.

Action Waves

Specific minion wave that serves as your execution timer

What Are Action Waves?

Short Definition:

The specific minion wave that serves as your team's execution timer

Detailed:

Instead of saying "move at 4:45" while everyone is focused on farming, you say "we go when wave 7 crashes." This transforms chaotic macro calls into precise coordination.

Why Waves Work Better

  • ‚úì Players already track minions for CS and lane management
  • ‚úì Waves provide natural "ready checks" - wrong position = not ready
  • ‚úì Creates automatic coordination - when wave moves, everyone moves
  • ‚úì Accounts for travel time and positioning naturally

30-Second Metronome System

Wave 7: 4:30

Calculation Steps

Step 1: Identify objective time (e.g., 5:00 Drake)
Step 2: Subtract 30 seconds (4:30 positioning)
Step 3: Find corresponding wave (Wave 7)
Step 4: Count backwards for setup

Lane Speed Differences

Mid Lane (Fastest)

  • ‚Ä¢ Waves crash faster
  • ‚Ä¢ More action wave opportunities
  • ‚Ä¢ Quick rotations to objectives
  • ‚Ä¢ Better for rapid plays

Bot Lane (Medium)

  • ‚Ä¢ Longer push/crash time
  • ‚Ä¢ Fewer wave opportunities
  • ‚Ä¢ Slower objective rotations
  • ‚Ä¢ Better for extended setups

Top Lane (Slowest)

  • ‚Ä¢ Slowest wave crashes
  • ‚Ä¢ Most isolated position
  • ‚Ä¢ TP-dependent timing
  • ‚Ä¢ Limited early impact

Common Action Wave Mistakes

‚ùå Wrong Wave Calculation

Thinking you have more time than you do

Example: Calling wave 8 for 5:00 objective (too late)

‚ùå Lane Mismatch

Using bot timing for mid plays

Solution: Match action wave to primary lane

‚ùå Solo Action Wave

One player moving, others not ready

Always confirm everyone's timing

‚ùå No Backup Plan

Missing action wave with no alternative

Always have next wave as backup

Action Wave Chains

Concept: Using one action wave to set up the next

Wave 4 Support roams for vision

Each wave builds toward the final execution.

Execution

Backup Plan

"Wave 6 failed, reset to wave 7. Everyone get ready."

5-Step Action Plan

Systematic approach to executing coordinated team plays

1

IDENTIFY (2 minutes before)

Define the objective and calculate your action wave timing

Objective time - 30 seconds = Execute Wave. Count backwards for preparation phases.

üí° Examples:

  • ‚úì 5:00 Dragon ‚Üí Execute on Wave 6 ‚Üí Setup starts at Wave 3-4
  • ‚úì Calculate precise timing for team coordination
  • ‚úì Identify which lane will be primary for execution
2

ASSIGN LANES (As soon as necessary)

Assign roles based on distance and rotation priority

Use distance priority rule to determine who moves first and who supports.

üí° Examples:

  • ‚úì Closest to Objective: Duo bot lane (shortest rotate)
  • ‚úì Medium Distance: Mid + Jungle (flexible support)
  • ‚úì Furthest: Top lane (TP dependent or split pressure)
3

PREPARE LANES (1 minute before Execute Wave)

Set up wave states for optimal positioning

Each role must prepare their lane state to enable the team play.

üí° Examples:

  • ‚úì Pushing: Leave lane without losing CS
  • ‚úì Crashed: Enemy chooses between objective and farm
  • ‚úì Bouncing Back: Enemy locked in lane, can't rotate
4

VISION (1 minute to 30 seconds before)

Control vision around the objective area

Support and jungle work together to secure vision control.

üí° Examples:

  • ‚úì Support: Place key wards, deny enemy vision spots
  • ‚úì Jungle: Clear enemy vision, secure safe pathways
  • ‚úì Control flanks and escape routes for team safety
5

RE-PREPARE & MOVE (Final positioning)

Execute final positioning and movement coordination

Coordinate final movement timing based on travel distance.

üí° Examples:

  • ‚úì 40 seconds: If recalling from lane (recall + walk time)
  • ‚úì 30 seconds: If walking from lane (direct rotate)
  • ‚úì 10 seconds: Final positioning adjustments only
